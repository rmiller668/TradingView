# Risk Language — TDF/RAF Framework Vocabulary

This document defines the language used across all TDF/RAF analysis, commentary, and documentation. When writing about market conditions, indicator states, or trading context, use these definitions and phrases consistently. This vocabulary is non-emotional, non-predictive, and structurally consistent.

---

## Anchor Principle

Risk moves through a progression:

> **Risk is rejected → accepted → agreed with → rewarded.**

The RAF Matrix measures where an asset sits in this progression across multiple time horizons.

## RAF Anchor Question

> *"Is the market paying me more, the same, or less to hold this risk — and is that changing?"*

---

## Core Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Risk** | Exposure to uncertain outcomes requiring capital, time, or volatility tolerance. |
| **Risk Rejection** | Insufficient willingness to hold risk at current prices, forcing price lower to clear exposure. |
| **Risk Acceptance** | Willingness to tolerate risk at current prices without requiring further price discount. |
| **Risk Agreement** | Broad alignment to hold and add risk, allowing price to advance with ease. |
| **Risk Reward** | Net positive payoff received for holding risk over time. |
| **Risk Penalty** | Net negative payoff imposed for holding risk. |
| **Risk Efficiency** | The amount of reward (or penalty) received per unit of risk taken. |
| **Rising Risk Cost** | Increasing effort, time, or exposure required for diminishing reward. |
| **Falling Risk Cost** | Decreasing effort required as reward efficiency improves. |
| **Risk Carry Cost** | The opportunity, volatility, and time cost of maintaining exposure while marginal reward declines. |
| **Crowded Risk** | Risk widely held, making exits slower and more contested. |
| **Risk Compression** | Reduced volatility and price movement indicating tolerance without commitment. |
| **Risk Expansion** | Increased volatility and directional movement indicating repricing or agreement. |
| **Risk Repair** | Phase where penalties shrink before rewards return. |
| **Risk Regime** | Sustained environment defining whether risk is rewarded, neutral, or penalized. |

---

## The Four RAF Questions (in order)

RAF enforces a strict question order. Each question must be answered before the next becomes relevant. Earlier questions filter environments; later questions refine participation only after risk has passed initial qualification.

1. **Is risk being rewarded?** — The first gate. If capital is not being rewarded for carrying uncertainty, participation should be limited or avoided.
2. **Is risk being accepted?** — Is reward supported by genuine acceptance, or merely temporary tolerance?
3. **How demanding is the risk?** — How difficult is it to remain exposed? Is uncertainty calm or unstable?
4. **How forgiving are the ownership terms?** — How sensitive is exposure to disappointment at this price?

Reading the RAF Matrix out of order converts it into an indicator dashboard. Reading it in order preserves it as a risk-governance tool.

## Language by Risk State

### Risk Rejection
- "Risk is being rejected"
- "Price must move to find holders"
- "Exposure is being forced out"
- "Capital has withdrawn"

### Risk Acceptance
- "Risk is tolerated here"
- "The market is no longer rejecting exposure"
- "This is an acceptance zone"
- "Participation is forming"

### Risk Agreement
- "Risk is agreed with"
- "The market is aligned"
- "Price is being carried higher"
- "Participation is broad and durable"

### Risk Efficiency
- "Efficiency is improving"
- "Efficiency is compressing"
- "Effort is outpacing reward"
- "Marginal risk is underpaid"

### Carry Cost & Crowding
- "Carry cost is rising"
- "Risk is crowded"
- "Exits are likely to be contested"
- "New risk is being paid less than old risk"

### Risk Repair
- "Risk repair is underway"
- "Penalty is diminishing"
- "This is repair, not agreement"
- "Losses are shrinking before price turns"

### Regime-Level
- "The regime is supportive"
- "The regime is hostile"
- "Risk is mispriced across horizons"
- "Short-term and long-term efficiencies are aligned / diverging"

## Key Distinctions

**Reward is feedback, not a promise.** Positive performance does not guarantee future compensation. Reward only tells you how the market has treated risk up to this point.

**Acceptance is not excitement.** Excitement reflects urgency and attention. Acceptance reflects willingness and durability. Markets can move rapidly on excitement without risk being genuinely accepted.

**Energy describes difficulty, not opportunity.** High energy means risk is harder to hold and mistakes are punished faster. Rising ATR is not a signal to act — it is a signal that the environment is becoming more demanding.

**Position refines risk, it does not qualify it.** A forgiving price location does not justify participation if risk is not being rewarded. Position is always the last question, never the first.

**More participation does not mean less risk.** When many participants hold similar exposure at similar prices, tolerance narrows and the system becomes more sensitive to disappointment. Crowding is different risk, not reduced risk.

---

## RAF Matrix Reading Discipline

- Always read **top to bottom**: Reward → Participation → Energy → Position
- Always read **right to left** (BASIS → 1X → 3X → 5X → 10X): current truth first, then structural support
- **Alignment** across dimensions and horizons = coherent risk conditions
- **Conflict** across dimensions = fragility; one dimension cannot override a disqualifying condition in another
- **Transition** = divergence appearing before price change; calls for awareness, not urgency

The RAF Matrix does not produce decisions. It produces clarity. When conditions are demanding, crowded, or unstable, the correct response is often restraint — not refinement or aggression.
