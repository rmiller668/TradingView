# Compensation Language Reference

## Purpose

This document establishes the standard language used within the Markets as Compensation Systems framework and the Risk Compensation Gauge (RCG).

The objective is to describe market conditions through the lens of compensation rather than prediction. Traditional market language often carries implied forecasts or directional bias. The compensation framework focuses instead on observing the quality of participation being offered by the market.

---

# Foundational Principle

**Price determines the cost of participation. Compensation determines the quality of participation.**

---

# Core Observation

**The chart is not a history of prices. The chart is a history of compensation environments.**

Price records the cost participants paid.

Compensation records the quality of participation participants received.

---

# Compensation State

Compensation State describes the current quality of participation as measured by Trend Compensation.

## Positive Compensation State

Participation is being rewarded.

Characteristics:

* Trend Compensation greater than zero.
* Compensation environment favorable for participants carrying risk.
* Represented by Blue (#90BFF9).

---

## Transitional Compensation State

Compensation is being repriced.

Characteristics:

* Trend Compensation near zero.
* Compensation advantage is unclear.
* Market determining whether compensation will improve or deteriorate.
* Represented by Gray (#4A4A4A).

---

## Negative Compensation State

Participation is being punished.

Characteristics:

* Trend Compensation less than zero.
* Compensation environment unfavorable for participants carrying risk.
* Represented by White (#FFFFFF).

---

# Compensation Direction

Compensation Direction describes how the quality of participation is changing.

## Compensation Improving

Basis Compensation is greater than Trend Compensation.

Interpretation:

Compensation is becoming more favorable.

---

## Compensation Stable

Basis Compensation is approximately equal to Trend Compensation.

Interpretation:

Compensation is relatively unchanged.

---

## Compensation Deteriorating

Basis Compensation is less than Trend Compensation.

Interpretation:

Compensation is becoming less favorable.

---

# Compensation Environment

Compensation Environment is the combination of Compensation State and Compensation Direction.

Examples:

* Positive Compensation State, Improving
* Positive Compensation State, Deteriorating
* Transitional Compensation State, Improving
* Transitional Compensation State, Deteriorating
* Negative Compensation State, Improving
* Negative Compensation State, Deteriorating

The Compensation Environment provides a complete description of current participation conditions.

---

# Preferred Terminology

| Preferred Language              | Meaning                              |
| ------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| Positive Compensation State     | Participation is being rewarded      |
| Transitional Compensation State | Compensation is being repriced       |
| Negative Compensation State     | Participation is being punished      |
| Compensation Improving          | Compensation becoming more favorable |
| Compensation Stable             | Compensation relatively unchanged    |
| Compensation Deteriorating      | Compensation becoming less favorable |
| Compensation Environment        | Current compensation condition       |
| Participation Rewarded          | Favorable compensation received      |
| Participation Punished          | Unfavorable compensation received    |

---

# Terms To Avoid

These terms are intentionally avoided because they often imply prediction rather than observation.

| Avoid              | Typical Implied Meaning      |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------- |
| Bullish            | Price should rise            |
| Bearish            | Price should fall            |
| Overbought         | Price should decline         |
| Oversold           | Price should rise            |
| Momentum           | Continue buying              |
| Weakness           | Continue selling             |
| Buyers In Control  | Directional assumption       |
| Sellers In Control | Directional assumption       |
| Trend Following    | Price-centric interpretation |

---

# Standard Interpretations

Instead of:

"NVDA is bullish."

Use:

"NVDA is in a Positive Compensation State."

---

Instead of:

"NVDA is bullish but momentum is weakening."

Use:

"NVDA remains in a Positive Compensation State, though compensation is deteriorating."

---

Instead of:

"GIS is bearish."

Use:

"GIS is in a Negative Compensation State."

---

Instead of:

"GIS may be forming a bottom."

Use:

"GIS remains in a Negative Compensation State, but compensation is improving."

---

# Framework Hierarchy

Price
: What is the cost of participation?

Basis Compensation
: What is the current compensation?

Trend Compensation
: What is the established compensation?

Compensation State
: What is the quality of participation?

Compensation Direction
: How is that quality changing?

Compensation Environment
: What are the overall participation conditions?

---

# Summary

Compensation State tells us where compensation is.

Compensation Direction tells us where compensation is moving.

Compensation Environment tells us the overall quality of participation being offered by the market.
