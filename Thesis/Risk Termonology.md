# *Markets are about risk exposure — which is either rewarded or penalized for carrying that exposure over time.  Price is the mechanism through which that cost is determined.*

## Risk Language Definitions

- **Risk**  
  Exposure to uncertain outcomes requiring capital, time, or volatility tolerance.

- **Risk Rejection**  
  Insufficient willingness to hold risk at current prices, forcing price lower to clear exposure.

- **Risk Acceptance**  
  Willingness to tolerate risk at current prices without requiring further price discount.

- **Risk Agreement**  
  Broad alignment to hold and add risk, allowing price to advance with ease.

- **Risk Reward**  
  Net positive payoff received for holding risk over time.

- **Risk Penalty**  
  Net negative payoff imposed for holding risk.

- **Risk Efficiency**  
  The amount of reward (or penalty) received per unit of risk taken.

- **Rising Risk Cost**  
  Increasing effort, time, or exposure required for diminishing reward.

- **Falling Risk Cost**  
  Decreasing effort required as reward efficiency improves.

- **Risk Carry Cost**  
  The opportunity, volatility, and time cost of maintaining exposure while marginal reward declines.

- **Crowded Risk**  
  Risk widely held, making exits slower and more contested.

- **Risk Compression**  
  Reduced volatility and price movement indicating tolerance without commitment.

- **Risk Expansion**  
  Increased volatility and directional movement indicating repricing or agreement.

- **Risk Repair**  
  Phase where penalties shrink before rewards return.

- **Risk Regime**  
  Sustained environment defining whether risk is rewarded, neutral, or penalized.

---

**Anchor Principle:**  
Risk is rejected → accepted → agreed with → rewarded.

## RAF – Risk-Oriented Questions & Phrases

### Core Orientation Questions
- Is risk being **rewarded or penalized**?
- Is the **cost of carrying risk rising or falling**?
- Is effort yielding **increasing or diminishing reward**?
- Is price moving because of **acceptance or force**?

---

### Risk Rejection Questions
- Is price being forced lower to clear holders?
- Are declines **fast and directional**?
- Is time insufficient to resolve imbalance?
- Is exposure being **punished**?

**Common phrases**
- “Risk is being rejected”
- “Price must move to find holders”
- “Exposure is being forced out”

---

### Risk Acceptance Questions
- Has price stopped declining without advancing?
- Are closes overlapping and ranges compressing?
- Has volatility contracted?
- Is time replacing price as the adjustment mechanism?

**Common phrases**
- “Risk is tolerated here”
- “The market is no longer rejecting exposure”
- “This is an acceptance zone”

---

### Risk Agreement Questions
- Is price advancing with ease?
- Are higher prices accepted and defended?
- Is participation expanding in the same direction?
- Is volatility expanding constructively?

**Common phrases**
- “Risk is agreed with”
- “The market is aligned”
- “Price is being carried higher”

---

### Risk Efficiency Questions
- Is performance improving or deteriorating?
- Is marginal reward higher or lower than the regime average?
- Is efficiency turning before price?
- Is reward keeping pace with effort?

**Common phrases**
- “Efficiency is improving”
- “Efficiency is compressing”
- “Effort is outpacing reward”

---

### Carry Cost & Crowding Questions
- Is new risk being paid less than old risk?
- Is participation elevated relative to reward?
- Are exits likely to be contested?
- Is time now working against the position?

**Common phrases**
- “Carry cost is rising”
- “Risk is crowded”
- “Marginal risk is underpaid”

---

### Repair & Transition Questions
- Are losses shrinking before price turns?
- Is efficiency improving below zero?
- Has downside pressure weakened?
- Is this repair or confirmation?

**Common phrases**
- “Risk repair is underway”
- “Penalty is diminishing”
- “This is repair, not agreement”

---

### Regime-Level Questions
- Is this a **rewarded**, **neutral**, or **penalized** regime?
- Are short-term and long-term efficiencies aligned?
- Is divergence expanding or compressing across horizons?

**Common phrases**
- “The regime is supportive”
- “The regime is hostile”
- “Risk is mispriced across horizons”

---
