## Core Philosophy

The Truth Detection Framework (TDF) is built on a single premise: lasting edge in markets comes from detecting when conditions are genuinely shifting, not from holding through time or chasing momentum. The tools in this workspace are designed to surface moments where the math — across price, volatility, and volume — collectively signals that value is present and participation is building, before price fully reflects it.

---

## Configuration 1 — The Two-Layer Macro + Cycle System

**Chart Setup:** TradingView split view — Monthly (1M) left, Weekly (1W) right

**Indicators:**
- Monthly: RAF Matrix, basis = 12
- Weekly: TDF Matrix, %P = 52, %B = 26, %V = 52

---

### The Thesis

This configuration builds a two-layer truth detection system — one that reads the macro risk environment, and one that reads individual asset structure within it.

---

### Layer 1 — Monthly RAF Matrix: Is the environment worth being in?

The monthly chart with RAF Matrix basis 12 gives you a 10-year structural view of risk. Before looking at any individual stock, you know the answer to the most important question: *is the market currently rewarding risk-takers?*

This is the **permission layer**. When the RAF Matrix shows RISK EXPANDING — efficiency positive, participation broad, energy calm — you have macro tailwind. When it shows RISK CROWDED or RISK IN TRANSITION, the environment is fragile even if individual setups look clean. When it shows RISK REJECTED, there is no need to look at the weekly chart at all.

The monthly chart doesn't lie about regime. It's too slow to be manipulated by short-term noise. What it shows is structural truth.

---

### Layer 2 — Weekly TDF Matrix (52, 26, 52): Where is this asset in its cycle?

With %P and %V at 52 weeks and %B at 26, the weekly TDF Matrix is calibrated to a full-year structural baseline. This is not a short-term swing setup finder — it's a cycle position reader. A low TDF Rating here means the asset is genuinely depressed relative to a full year of price, volatility, and volume history. That's a meaningful statement.

The 52-week basis also aligns naturally with how institutional money thinks — annual performance cycles, 52-week highs and lows as reference points, annual volume patterns. You're reading the asset on the same clock that large capital uses.

---

### The Combined Read

The power of this configuration is the **alignment filter**. Act only when both layers agree:

| Monthly RAF State | Weekly TDF | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| RISK EXPANDING | Low rating, %V building | Highest conviction — macro supports, asset is at value |
| RISK STABILIZING | Low rating, %V building | Good setup, macro repairing — size smaller, confirm before adding |
| RISK EXPANDING | High TDF Rating | Macro is fine but asset is extended — wait for pullback |
| RISK CROWDED | Low TDF Rating | Asset looks cheap but macro is fragile — avoid or minimal starter |
| RISK REJECTED | Any | Don't trade. The environment is not compensating risk. |

---

### The Setup This Configuration Is Designed to Find

An asset that has been in a prolonged decline or consolidation — TDF Rating low across all timeframes on the weekly — while the monthly RAF Matrix is in RISK EXPANDING or transitioning from RISK STABILIZING to RISK EXPANDING. That combination means the macro environment is healthy and this specific asset is lagging. Laggards in healthy environments tend to catch up.

This is not a trade signal. It is a candidate for deeper investigation. The trigger still comes from price action on the chart. But the two-layer filter means that by the time you're looking at the chart, the math on both timeframes has already confirmed the odds are favorable.

### Basis Period Rationale

| Timeframe | Indicator | %P | %B | %V | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Monthly | RAF Matrix | 12 | — | — | 1x horizon = 1 year; 10x = 10 years |
| Weekly | TDF Matrix | 52 | 26 | 52 | 1x horizon = 1 year; maintains 2:1 %P:%B ratio |

The 2:1 ratio between %P and %B is intentional — %B is kept more responsive than %P to preserve the sensitivity differential that makes the two metrics complementary rather than redundant.
